WORM VEIL AI is built from the ground up around one principle: no one — not the platform, not any server in the chain, not a passive network observer — should be able to identify you or read what you send. Every request you make passes through a stack of overlapping protections before it ever reaches the AI model, and every response travels back through the same stack in reverse.

## Traffic Routing

Your messages never travel directly to the AI. Instead, they move through a three-node chain specifically designed to sever the link between your identity and your queries.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Amsterdam Gateway — Encryption & Tor Entry">
    Your browser connects first to the Amsterdam Gateway. This node accepts your connection over **WireGuard** and applies **AES-256-GCM + ChaCha20** encryption before wrapping the traffic in **TLS 1.3** with Perfect Forward Secrecy. It also acts as the **Tor Entry Node**, injecting your traffic into the Tor network so that no downstream node ever sees your real IP address. The gateway keeps zero logs.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Singapore Balancer — Proxy & DDoS Shield">
    Traffic arrives at the Singapore Balancer as an already-anonymised Tor circuit. This node distributes load across the infrastructure, applies rate-limiting DDoS protections, and routes the request through a **3-hop SOCKS5 proxy chain** for an additional layer of network-level separation. No connection metadata is retained.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Panama Main Host — Inference Only">
    The fully anonymised request reaches the Panama Main Host, where the **Dolphin 3.0 Abliterated 8B** model runs on 4× NVIDIA A100 80 GB GPUs backed by 512 GB RAM. This node processes the prompt entirely in RAM, generates a response, and immediately discards all data. Nothing is written to disk at any point in this pipeline.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The full path uses **WebSocket over Tor**, **DNS-over-HTTPS** to prevent DNS leakage, and **Certificate Pinning** to block man-in-the-middle interception at the TLS layer.

## Browser Fingerprint Protection

Even with encrypted, anonymised traffic, a website can often identify you through your browser's unique fingerprint — a combination of subtle signals your browser emits by default. WORM VEIL AI disables or spoofs each of these vectors automatically.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="WebRTC Disabled" icon="wifi-slash" href="/security/encryption">
    WebRTC can expose your real IP address even when you are behind a VPN or Tor, because it establishes peer-to-peer connections that bypass proxy settings. WORM VEIL disables WebRTC entirely so your real IP is never leaked through this channel.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Canvas Noise" icon="paintbrush" href="/security/encryption">
    Browsers render canvas elements in subtly different ways depending on your GPU, drivers, and OS — creating a highly stable fingerprint. WORM VEIL injects randomised noise into canvas read-back calls so each session produces a different canvas hash.
  </Card>
  <Card title="AudioContext Noise" icon="waveform" href="/security/encryption">
    The Web Audio API generates device-specific output that trackers use to fingerprint hardware. WORM VEIL adds imperceptible noise to AudioContext values, making your audio fingerprint unique and non-repeatable across sessions.
  </Card>
  <Card title="WebGL Vendor Spoofing" icon="microchip" href="/security/encryption">
    WebGL exposes your GPU vendor and renderer strings, which are stable and identifying. WORM VEIL spoofs these strings so your graphics hardware cannot be used to track you across visits.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Fake User-Agent" icon="browser" href="/security/encryption">
    Your browser's User-Agent string reveals your browser version, operating system, and sometimes device type. WORM VEIL substitutes a randomised User-Agent to prevent this information from being correlated with your identity.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## What Is Not Logged

Every node in the WORM VEIL infrastructure operates under a strict zero-log policy. The following data is **never recorded anywhere**:

- **IP addresses** — neither your real IP nor the Tor exit node IP is stored at any point in the chain.
- **Message content** — prompts and responses exist only in RAM during active inference and are discarded immediately after the response is returned.
- **Timestamps** — no record is kept of when a request was made or how long it took.
- **User behaviour** — session duration, agent mode selection, number of messages, and navigation patterns are not tracked.
- **User identifiers** — your user ID is generated and stored exclusively in your own browser; the servers never see or store it.

<Note>
  Your conversation history and user ID are stored only in your browser's **localStorage**. They never leave your device and are never transmitted to or stored on any WORM VEIL server. If you clear your browser data or use a different device, that history is gone.
</Note>

<Tip>
  For the strongest possible anonymity, access WORM VEIL AI through the **Tor Browser** or pair it with a trusted **no-log VPN**. This adds an independent anonymity layer on top of WORM VEIL's own Tor routing, giving you protection even in the unlikely event of a partial infrastructure compromise.
</Tip>
